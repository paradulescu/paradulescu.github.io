About

Aerospace engineering foundations, applied through optimization and ML

My background is in aerospace engineering and applied physics, with a focus on computational optimization. I hold a dual MSE and BSE in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Michigan (4.0 MSE GPA, 3.87 BSE GPA, Summa Cum Laude), built on numerical methods, CFD (Navier–Stokes and Euler), finite element analysis, and structural, modal, and thermal analysis. That foundation carried into my role at large aerospace company like Lockheed Martin.

Today I apply that background at the intersection of simulation and machine learning at PhysicsX. I build production ML pipelines for CAE and physics systems, and an automated active learning pipeline. I translate first principles physics into ML feature engineering to improve surrogate model accuracy, and the tools I have shipped are now used across every delivery project on the platform.

Engineering at PX

What it looks like day to day

I own end to end simulation workflows: generating and curating large-scale physics datasets (structural, modal/vibration, thermal) across electronics packaging, semiconductors, turbine blades, and battery thermal runaway, then building preprocessing pipelines and simulation outputs that other engineers can use directly, cutting hand-off work. This is the layer underneath every ML model we ship, and it is where I spend my time translating first principles physics into something a model, or another engineer, can act on.
