About

Physics meets data science

I'm a simulation engineer with a strong aerospace foundation, currently at PhysicsX in New York, where I develop ML surrogate models that accelerate high-fidelity physics simulations for automotive and electronics clients.

My background spans CFD, FEA, propulsion, and space systems — from F-35 inlet studies at Lockheed Martin Aeronautics to Thrust Vector Actuator optimization at Lockheed Martin Space. I hold a dual MSE/BS in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Michigan (4.0 MSE GPA, Summa Cum Laude).

I'm passionate about compressing engineering iteration cycles through intelligent automation — turning 90-hour simulations into 12-hour workflows, and building the tooling to make physics-driven ML accessible at scale.
