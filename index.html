About

Engineer first, growing into data science

I'm a simulation and aerospace engineer currently at PhysicsX in New York, where I own end-to-end simulation workflows as the sole simulation engineer on my projects and work alongside data science and software teams to develop ML surrogate models that accelerate high-fidelity physics simulations.

My background is grounded in CFD, FEA, propulsion, and space systems — from transonic inlet studies at Lockheed Martin Aeronautics to Thrust Vector Actuator optimization at Lockheed Martin Space. I hold a dual MSE/BS in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Michigan (4.0 MSE GPA, Summa Cum Laude).

I'm increasingly focused on the intersection of traditional simulation and machine learning — translating simulation physics into ML feature engineering, building automated pipelines, and acting as the technical bridge across simulation, data science, and software engineering.
